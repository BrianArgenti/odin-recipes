Oyakodon

Description

Oyakodon is a simple Japanese dish that consists of chicken, egg, and vegtables on rice. "Oya" means parent and "ko" means child in japanese in reference to the parent chicken and child egg from which the dish's name is derived. "Don" is short for "donburi" which is a reference to the bowl the dish is served in. Other donburi dishes include "gyuudon", beef bowl and "butadon", pork bowl. Itadakimasu!

Ingredients

  • Vegetable Oil: 1 tsp
  • De-boned chicken: 3/4 lb
  • onion sliced: 1/2
  • chicken broth: 1 cup
  • shiitake mushrooms: 6
  • carrot cut into matchsticks: 1
  • sugar: 2 tbsp
  • shoyu: 4 tbsp
  • salt: 1/2 tsp
  • green onions chopped: 1/2 cup
  • eggs beaten: 5

Steps

  1. Heat oil in skillet med-hi heat. Saute chicken and onions, drain liquid.
  2. Stir in broth, simmer for 2 min. Add mushroom and carrots.
  3. After a few minutes add sugar, shoyu, and salt. Simmer 3 min.
  4. Sprinkle half of green onion in and stir. Pour eggs over the top and simmer until eggs are cooked.
  5. Serve over rice.
